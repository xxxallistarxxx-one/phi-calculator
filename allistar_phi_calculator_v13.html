The Great Pyramid of Giza — Encoded Mathematics

The Great Pyramid encodes both phi and pi in its proportions simultaneously. The apothem (slant height of face) divided by half the base = phi = 1.618. The perimeter divided by twice the height = pi = 3.14159. It also encodes sqrt(phi) as the height/half-base ratio. Original dimensions: base 230.33m (440 cubits), height 146.5m (280 cubits).
